Tips to reduce weight

There are many ways to lose a lot of weight fast

However, most of them will mae you hungry and unsatisfied

Below are some tips that will make you lose weight quickly, without hunger.

  • Cut Back on Sugars and Starches
  • Eat Protein, Fat and Vegetables

    • Check protein sources

  • Meat:Beef, Chicken, pork, lamb, etc.
  • Fish and Seafood:Salmon, trout, shrimp, etc.
  • Eggs:Whole eggs with the yolk are best.
  • Dairy Products:Like milk, yoghurt and cheese.
  • Soy Products:Like tofu.
  • Beans and legumes:Such as lentils and chickpeas.
  • Grain and Cereal Products:These are generally not as high in protein as meat and meat alternative products..
  • Lift Weights 3 Times Per Week

Natural ways to lower your blood pressure

High blood pressure is a dangerous condition that can damage your heart. It affects one in three people in the US and 1 billion people worldwide.

Here are some ways to combat high blood pressure.

  • Walk and excercise regularly
  • Reduce your sodium intake
  • Drink less alcohol
  • Eat more potassium-rich foods
  • Cut back on caffeine

    • Some more ways

  • Learn to manage stress
  • Eat dark chocolate or cocoa
  • Lose weight
  • Quit smoking
  • Cut added sugar and refined carbs
  • Eat berries
  • Try meditation or deep breathing
  • Eat calcium-rich foods
  • Take natural supplements
  • Eat foods rich in magnesium

Ways to lower blood sugar levels

High blood sugar occurs when your body can't effectively transport sugar from blood into cells. When left unchecked, this can lead to diabetes. One study from 2012 reported that 12-14% of US adults had type 2 diabetes, while 37-38% were classified as pre-diabetic

Here are some ways to combat high sugar levels.

  • Exercise Regularly
  • Control your carb intake
  • Increase your fiber intake
  • Drink water and stay hydrated
  • Implement portion control

    • Some more ways

  • Choose foods with a low glycemic index
  • Control stress levels
  • Monitor your blood sugar levels
  • Get enough quality sleep
  • Eat foods rich in chromium and magnesium
  • Try Apple Cider Vinegar
  • Experiment with cinnamon extract
  • Try berberine
  • Eat fenugreek seeds

Frequently Asked Questions

Find the answer to your questions.

What does BMI stand for?

  • Body mass index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight that applies to adult men and women.
  • Want to have a quick BMI test? Click on this link:BMI Calculator

    • What are blood sugar level for the majority of healthy individuals?

  • Between 4.0 to 5.4 mmol/L (72 to 99 mg/dL) when fasting
  • Up to 7.8 mmol/L (140 mg/dL) 2 hours after eating
  • Want to check if you are in the normal range? Click on this link:Glucose Checker

    • What are blood sugar level for people with diabetes?

  • Before meals: 4 to 7 mmol/L for people with type 1 or type 2 diabetes
  • After meals: under 9 mmol/L for people with type 1 diabetes and under 8.5mmol/L for people with type 2 diabetes
  • Want to check if you are in the normal range? Click on this link:Glucose Checker

    • What does mmol/L and mg/dL stand for?

  • mmol/LInternational standard unit for measuring the concentration of glucose in the blood - also known as millimolar (mM). This unit is used in the UK.: Millimoles per litre
  • mg/dLUnit for measuring concentration of glucose in the blood in the USA - milligrams per decilitre.: Milligrams per 100 millilitres

    • Whats the difference between mmol/L and mg/dL?

  • Both sets of units are used to measure blood sugar levels and both give a measurement of the concentration of glucose in the blood, albeit in slightly different ways.
  • mmol/L gives the molarity, which is the number of molecules of a substance within a specified volume, in this case within 1 litre.
  • mg/dL gives the concentration by the ratio of weight to volume, in this case milligrams per decilitre.
  • mmol/L is the most common measurement used in the UK with mg/dL predominantly used in the USA and continental Europe.
